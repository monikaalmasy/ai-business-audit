## Why Free for the Audit

The free audit is NOT charity. It is a calculated customer acquisition strategy with near-zero marginal cost.

**Cost breakdown per free audit:**
- Deepgram transcription (10 min average): $0.04
- Claude API calls (analysis + questionnaire + report): $0.55
- PDF generation and email delivery: $0.01
- File storage: $0.01
- Operator review time (15 min at imputed $50/hr): $12.50
- **Total hard cost: $0.70** (excluding operator time)
- **Total with operator time: $13.20**

**Value of a free audit to the client:**
- A traditional consultant would charge $200-500/hr for a discovery meeting
- A typical discovery process involves 2-3 meetings (4-6 hours) before delivering an audit report
- Total cost of equivalent service: $1,500-$3,000
- Our free audit delivers comparable or better analysis in 24 hours for $0.70 in API costs

**Why it works as a lead magnet:**
1. The report is genuinely useful -- even clients who never buy the paid tier walk away with actionable insights
2. The report demonstrates competence -- "if the free audit is this good, the paid build must be excellent"
3. The report creates urgency -- once a business owner sees they're wasting 40 hours/month on manual tasks, the cost of NOT automating becomes visible
4. The report is specific, not generic -- it references the client's own words, their own spreadsheets, their own tools. This makes it impossible to ignore.
5. Zero risk to the client -- no payment info required, no meeting to sit through, no commitment

**Expected conversion rate: 10%**
- At 100 free audits/month: 10 paid clients
- Customer acquisition cost: $0.70 x 100 / 10 = $7.00 per paying client (excluding operator time)
- With operator time: $13.20 x 100 / 10 = $132 per paying client
- For a $4,000 average deal, that's a 30:1 ROI on customer acquisition

## Why $3,000-$5,000 for the Automation Build

### The Self-Service Checkout Threshold

Pricing research and B2B SaaS benchmarks consistently show:
- **Under $5,000:** Business owners will self-serve. Application form + Stripe checkout works. No sales call needed.
- **$5,000-$15,000:** A short qualification call or Zoom is usually expected. Still possible without, but conversion drops.
- **Above $15,000:** Almost always requires a sales process (calls, proposals, negotiation).

We intentionally price below $5,000 to stay in the self-service zone. This means:
- No sales team needed
- No scheduling software
- No proposal templates
- No follow-up sequences
- No "let me check with my partner" delay

The entire sales process is: read the audit report -> click "Build This For Me" -> pay via Stripe -> done.

### Value-Based Justification

**What the client is buying:**
- 3-5 automations that save 30-60 hours/month
- At even a modest $25/hr (well below what most business owners value their time at), that's $750-$1,500/month in savings
- $3-5K pays for itself in 2-4 months
- Over 12 months: $9,000-$18,000 in savings from a $3-5K investment
- ROI: 200-400%

**What it costs us:**
- Claude API costs for solution building: $10-$30 per automation
- Operator review and Loom recording: 2-3 hours total ($100-$150 at imputed rate)
- Infrastructure costs: negligible
- Total cost per project: $200-$400
- **Gross margin: 90-95%**

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring
The free audit report shows the client that they're wasting $3,000-$5,000/month on manual tasks. When the "Build This For Me" CTA shows a $3-5K one-time price, it's anchored against the MONTHLY cost of not automating. The automation pays for itself in 1-2 months.

### Loss Aversion
The audit report quantifies what the client is LOSING every month by not automating. "You're spending 47 hours/month on tasks that could be automated. At $50/hr, that's $2,350/month walking out the door." This is more motivating than "you could save $2,350/month."

### Zero-Risk Entry
The free audit has no risk: no payment info, no meeting, no commitment. This gets the client into the pipeline. Once they've invested the time to record a voice note and complete a questionnaire, they're psychologically invested in the outcome.

### Social Proof (Future)
Once we have 10+ clients, add to the report: "We've helped [X] businesses save a combined [Y] hours/month." Every new client adds to the proof, which improves conversion for the next client.

## When to Raise Prices

Raise prices when:
1. **Conversion rate exceeds 15%** -- we're underpriced. Move to $5K-$8K for the build tier.
2. **Demand exceeds capacity** -- if the operator can't keep up with review, price up to reduce volume and increase revenue per client.
3. **Average savings per client exceeds $5K/month** -- if clients are routinely saving $5K+/month, a $3-5K one-time build is too cheap. Move to $7K-$10K.
4. **Enterprise clients start appearing** -- companies with 50+ employees have bigger budgets. Create an Enterprise tier at $10K-$20K with more automations and dedicated support.

Do NOT raise prices until at least 20 paid clients have been delivered successfully. Early stage is about learning, not maximizing revenue.
