# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Core Positioning

**The free audit IS the marketing.** Every distribution channel leads to the same action: get the business owner to record a voice note and receive their free AI audit report. The report sells the paid service. There is no sales team, no outbound calling, no slide decks. The product sells itself.

**Key message:** "Record a voice note about your biggest bottleneck. Get an AI-powered audit showing exactly what can be automated and how much you'll save. No meetings. No consultants. Just results."

## Target Market

### Primary: Small Businesses with 5-50 Employees

These businesses are:
- Large enough to have real operational pain (repetitive tasks, manual processes, communication bottlenecks)
- Small enough that they can't afford a $50K consulting engagement or a full-time operations manager
- Usually run by an owner-operator who is personally frustrated by inefficiency
- Making decisions quickly (no procurement committee, no 6-month evaluation cycle)
- Willing to spend $3-5K to solve a problem that costs them $3-5K/month in wasted time

### Target Industries (Prioritized)

1. **Professional Services (Accounting, Legal, Financial Planning)**
   - Pain: Client communication, document management, scheduling, billing follow-ups
   - Typical savings: 20-40 hours/month across a 10-person team
   - They understand ROI and respond to numbers
   - High lifetime value (likely to add retainer)

2. **Healthcare Administration (Clinics, Dental Offices, Allied Health)**
   - Pain: Appointment scheduling, patient follow-ups, insurance paperwork, staff coordination
   - Typical savings: 30-60 hours/month
   - Highly regulated -- they value compliance-aware solutions
   - Recurring revenue potential is high (always new compliance requirements)

3. **E-commerce Operations**
   - Pain: Order processing, inventory management, customer service, returns handling, social media
   - Typical savings: 25-50 hours/month
   - They're already tech-forward and open to automation
   - Seasonal spikes make automation especially valuable

4. **Real Estate (Agencies and Property Management)**
   - Pain: Listing management, client follow-ups, inspection scheduling, document preparation
   - Typical savings: 15-30 hours/month per agent
   - High transaction values mean even small efficiency gains have large dollar impact

5. **Trades and Home Services**
   - Pain: Quoting, scheduling, invoicing, follow-ups, review management
   - Typical savings: 15-25 hours/month
   - Often completely non-technical -- they'll pay for done-for-you solutions
   - Strong word-of-mouth referral networks

### Ideal Client Profile

- Revenue: $500K-$10M/year
- Team: 5-50 people
- Currently using: a messy combination of spreadsheets, email, maybe one or two SaaS tools they're not fully utilizing
- Decision maker: the owner or a COO/operations manager
- Trigger event: they just had a frustrating week where something fell through the cracks, or they're about to hire someone and wondering if they could automate instead

## Distribution Channels

### Channel 1: Content Engine (Spawni Cross-Promotion)

Once Spawni has content traction, the Spawni mascot and content engine promotes the audit service. This is the long-term organic growth channel.

- Blog posts: "5 Signs Your Business Needs an Automation Audit" / "How Much Is Manual Work Costing Your Business?"
- Social posts showing audit report examples (anonymized) with before/after metrics
- Short-form video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts): screen recordings of the intake process and report output
- Every piece of content ends with: "Get your free audit at [domain]"

### Channel 2: Cold Email (B2B Outreach)

Compliant B2B cold email to businesses with publicly listed email addresses. Same legal framework as any B2B SaaS outreach.

**Email structure:**
```
Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]

Hi [First Name],

I built an AI tool that analyzes business operations and identifies
automation opportunities. It's free -- you record a voice note about
your biggest bottleneck, and the AI generates a personalized report
showing what can be automated and how much you'll save.

No meetings, no sales calls. Just a report.

Would you be open to trying it? Takes about 5 minutes.

[CTA: Get Your Free Audit]
```

**Targeting:**
- Scrape business directories (Google Maps, Yelp, industry directories) for businesses in target industries and size ranges
- Use Apollo.io or similar for email finding
- Send 50-100 emails/day (warm up domain first)
- Expected response rate: 3-5%
- Expected audit completion rate: 30-50% of responses

### Channel 3: LinkedIn Content

Personal brand content on LinkedIn targeting business owners and operations managers.

**Content types:**
- Case studies: "This accounting firm was spending 40 hours/month on invoice follow-ups. Here's what we found in their audit." (with permission or anonymized)
- Educational: "3 things every small business should automate first"
- Behind-the-scenes: "How our AI audit works -- from voice note to report in 24 hours"
- Results: Screenshots of savings projections from real audits (anonymized)
- Opinion: "Why I stopped doing discovery meetings and replaced them with voice notes"

**Posting cadence:** 3-5 posts/week

### Channel 4: VSL (Video Sales Letter)

A 10-15 minute recorded video that explains the entire service, walks through the process, and shows real results.

**VSL structure:**
1. Hook: "What if you could get a $2,000 consulting audit for free, in 24 hours, without a single meeting?"
2. Problem: The pain of manual operations, wasted time, hiring people for tasks that should be automated
3. Solution: Walk through the AI Business Audit process (screen recording of the intake form, example voice note, example report)
4. Proof: Show 2-3 anonymized audit reports with real savings numbers
5. How it works: Step-by-step breakdown of the journey (intake -> questionnaire -> report -> build)
6. Pricing: Explain the free audit, the paid build tier, the retainer
7. Objection handling: "But what about my specific industry?" / "How do I know the AI understands my business?" / "What if I don't like the report?"
8. CTA: "Click below to get your free audit"

**Distribution:**
- Landing page (embedded above the fold or as a dedicated /watch page)
- YouTube (SEO for "business automation audit" / "AI business consulting")
- LinkedIn (native upload for better reach)
- Cold email follow-up (for non-responders: "I recorded a quick video explaining how this works")

### Channel 5: Application Form + Stripe Checkout

For the paid tier, no sales call needed at the $3-5K price point.

**Flow:**
1. Client receives audit report
2. Report includes "Build This For Me" CTA
3. Click -> Application page (simple form: confirm selected automations, timeline preferences, any questions)
4. Submit -> Stripe checkout
5. Payment -> Project begins

For retainer ($1.5-2.5K/month):
1. Offered at the end of the project delivery
2. Short application form: goals for ongoing optimization, preferred communication channel
3. Stripe subscription checkout
4. Monthly billing begins

### Channel 6: Word of Mouth and Referrals

The free audit is inherently shareable. Business owners talk to other business owners. The audit report is impressive enough that recipients share it.

**Referral program (Phase 2):**
- Refer a business that completes a free audit -> get a $50 Amazon gift card
- Refer a business that buys a paid tier -> get 10% of the project value (up to $500)
- Simple referral link system (unique URL per client)

## Launch Sequence

### Phase 1: MVP (Week 1-2)
- Deploy landing page with intake form
- Backend pipeline working end-to-end (voice -> transcription -> analysis -> questionnaire -> report)
- Manual operator review on all reports before sending
- No paid tier yet -- just the free audit
- Send to 10 warm contacts (friends, network, existing clients) for feedback

### Phase 2: Paid Tier (Week 3-4)
- Add Stripe checkout to reports
- Build the solution delivery pipeline
- Onboard first 3-5 paying clients from Phase 1 audit recipients
- Iterate on report quality based on feedback

### Phase 3: Outbound (Week 5-8)
- Start cold email campaigns (50-100/day)
- Begin LinkedIn content posting
- Record and deploy VSL
- Target: 20 free audits/week, 2-3 paid clients/month

### Phase 4: Scale (Month 3+)
- Cross-promote with Spawni content engine
- Launch referral program
- Expand to additional industries based on data
- Evaluate Spawni integration (if 10+ paying clients achieved)
- Consider hiring a VA for operator tasks (Loom recording, review)

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk: Low conversion from free audit to paid tier**
- Mitigation: Improve report quality, add more specific ROI projections, A/B test CTA copy
- Fallback: Offer a "Mini Build" tier at $997 (1 automation) as a lower commitment entry point

**Risk: Voice note quality is too poor for analysis**
- Mitigation: Add prompts/coaching on the intake page ("Tell us about a typical day", "What task frustrates you most?")
- Fallback: Offer a structured text intake form as an alternative

**Risk: Clients expect a meeting anyway**
- Mitigation: Set expectations clearly on the landing page and in all communications. "No meetings" is a feature, not a bug.
- Fallback: For clients above $5K, offer a single 30-minute Zoom call. But keep this as a premium exception, not the default.

**Risk: Solution quality doesn't meet expectations**
- Mitigation: Operator review on every deliverable. 30-day support window. Clear scope in the audit report.
- Fallback: Money-back guarantee on the paid tier (we keep the automations that do work, refund the portion that doesn't)
